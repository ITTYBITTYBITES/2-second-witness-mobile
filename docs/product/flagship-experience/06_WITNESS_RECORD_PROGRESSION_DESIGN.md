# Witness Record Progression Design

**Goal:** make personal continuity meaningful without currencies, artificial rewards, badge clutter, or claims about real-world ability.

---

# 1. Progression principle

The player should not progress because they accumulated numbers. They should progress because the product remembers a growing relationship with witnessed moments.

> **The Witness Record is a private record of scenes noticed, evidence understood, and in-game observation familiarity built over time.**

It is not a cognitive score, diagnosis, leaderboard rank, or productivity tracker.

---

# 2. The Witness Record: three layers

## Layer A — Current Brief

**Question it answers:** *What is my current moment or finite session?*

This is the primary progression signal during ordinary use.

It should communicate only:

- current Witness Brief state;
- completed moments in the brief;
- whether there is a clear next/resume moment;
- clean completion when the brief ends.

The existing Program state, unfinished-run Continue, daily rotation, and result metadata are usable building blocks.

**Rule:** a missed day never breaks or punishes the player. A brief is fresh when it is fresh; it is not a debt.

## Layer B — Witness Familiarity

**Question it answers:** *What parts of this game am I becoming familiar with?*

This layer replaces a generic “level up for points” feeling with meaningful in-game familiarity:

- scene worlds encountered: Office, Kitchen, Workshop, Travel Desk, Garden Bench;
- observation relationships experienced: presence, count, attribute, position, adjacency, container/region;
- companion modes discovered after the flagship is understood;
- fair difficulty familiarity over time.

Existing per-family mastery, content metadata, template IDs, question history, and player history can support this. Scene-specific/content-tag records may need additive content metadata; no currency/inventory system is required.

## Layer C — Moment Archive

**Question it answers:** *What moments have I witnessed?*

The archive is a reflective history, not a spreadsheet. It can retain:

- recent moments and scene category;
- the evidence/reveal that made the answer meaningful;
- correct/missed outcome in non-shaming language;
- favorite moments or preferred scene worlds;
- completed Witness Briefs.

The existing flattened Challenge History, family history, favorites, and Profile surfaces are a base. The future direction is to make history feel like remembered moments rather than raw event logs.

---

# 3. What mastery means

## Mastery definition

Mastery means **growing familiarity with fair observation in this game**. It should emerge from repeated, varied, completed Witness Moments—not only speed and not one lucky score.

A healthy mastery model considers:

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Completed varied moments | Familiarity should come from seeing more than one repeated seed/template. |
| Correctness over a reasonable window | Accuracy can show in-game understanding without judging one miss. |
| Evidence engagement/understanding | The reveal is the learning unit; the player should not be rewarded for skipping it. Research must determine a respectful measurement. |
| Scene/template diversity | Mastery of a scene world should not be one narrow exploit. |
| Chosen comfort/accessibility settings | Normal progress must remain available under Comfortable Timing, Reading Comfort, High Contrast, and relevant alternatives. |
| Voluntary return/completion | Consistent engagement can be recognized without punitive streak mechanics. |

## What mastery must not mean

- a claim that the player is objectively more intelligent, attentive, or diagnostically skilled;
- a pure response-speed score;
- a forced difficulty escalator;
- a reason to hide basic content behind opaque gates;
- a requirement to play every day;
- a badge/coin currency loop.

---

# 4. Ranks, levels, achievements, and collections

## Witness Level and Rank

Existing Witness Level/Rank infrastructure can remain, but it should serve as a calm summary of record depth rather than the main reward. It should not interrupt every result or make a player feel behind.

A rank is useful only if it has understandable meaning, such as expanded familiarity with the game’s ordinary scene worlds or readiness for more layered but still fair Witness Moments.

## Achievements

The existing 26 data-driven achievements are valuable infrastructure but should become **optional recognition**, not the primary progression map.

Use achievements when they mark a genuinely memorable relationship:

- first completed Witness Moment;
- first full brief;
- meaningful scene-world familiarity;
- a fair personal consistency milestone;
- discovery of a companion style the player genuinely chose.

Do not make players browse or optimize a long achievement catalog to understand the product.

## Collections

Current collections are derived counters. They should not become an inventory/economy unless real player research shows that collecting scene worlds or evidence moments deepens the witness fantasy. The default direction is to keep collections as optional reflective context.

---

# 5. Returning habit without streak pressure

## The desired habit

The player returns because there is a fresh moment worth noticing, not because yesterday’s number is threatened.

A healthy return message is conceptually:

> **Today’s Witness Brief is ready.**

An unhealthy return message is conceptually:

> **Do not lose your streak.**

## Existing systems to reuse

- Deterministic daily feature.
- Daily Witness Program.
- Program resume state.
- Recommendation service.
- Local date logic.
- Witness history and favorites.

## Directional rule

Keep a correct-answer streak as optional reflection if research shows players understand it. Do not use it as the central daily retention mechanism, and do not call it a daily streak unless it measures days.

---

# 6. Progression flow over time

| Time horizon | Player experience | Record behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **First two moments** | Learns the Witness Moment and sees proof. | Records first moment; avoids profile/dashboard overload. |
| **First week** | Returns to a few fresh briefs; begins to recognize scene worlds and question grammar. | Shows continuity/familiarity gently; companion discovery remains optional. |
| **Several weeks** | Has preferred scene worlds/modes, handles richer fair moments, recognizes personal in-game patterns. | Witness Record reflects breadth, favorite moments, and scene familiarity. |
| **Long term** | Uses the app as a private observation ritual; varies through companion modes when desired. | Archive/history becomes emotionally meaningful; mastery adapts content without claiming life improvement. |

---

# 7. Progression acceptance criteria

Before changing player-facing progression, research must establish that players:

- understand what the current brief means;
- can explain what their Witness Record represents;
- do not interpret rank/mastery as an external ability diagnosis;
- value at least one long-term signal beyond raw accuracy;
- do not feel pressured by a missed day or one incorrect answer;
- can find a past meaningful moment in the archive/history;
- see companion-mode discovery as optional enrichment, not required homework;
- progress normally using accessibility/comfort settings.

If these criteria fail, simplify the player-facing progression rather than adding another reward layer.
