# Player Experience Evolution Map

**Purpose:** map how player perception should evolve across the ten-update flagship roadmap.
**Use:** product/design/release teams should judge updates by the change in player feeling, not feature count.

---

# Starting perception

## Current baseline

> **“This is an interesting observation game.”**

A player can find five distinct challenge types, generated content, progress, Programs, accessibility controls, and an evidence reveal. The baseline has genuine depth, but the player may still perceive a set of modes, metrics, and options rather than one unmistakable flagship ritual.

| Baseline perception | Current strength | Current ambiguity |
|---|---|---|
| “Interesting observation game” | Core observe/recall/reveal works. | What is the one reason to return? |
| “There are several activities here” | Five families create variety. | Which one defines the product? |
| “It tracks my progress” | Local mastery/history/achievements exist. | What does progress mean emotionally? |
| “It has a dark premium look” | Brand/audio/assets/shell exist. | Does gameplay/store/trailer express one tone? |

# Per-update player journey

| Update | Player belief after update | Emotional shift | What the player should notice | What must not happen |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Witness Moment Foundation** | “This is different from normal puzzle games.” | Curiosity → focused attention. | One clear scene-based witness contract and fair first moment. | They should not feel tutorial burden, catalog confusion, or a test. |
| **2. Evidence Reveal Transformation** | “The reveal is the reason I play.” | Tension → realization. | Scene returns, evidence becomes visible, explanation makes truth clear. | Score, failure, badges, or animation should not dominate. |
| **3. Scene Quality Pipeline** | “Every scene feels intentional.” | Trust → sustained curiosity. | Distinct ordinary worlds, fair objects, readable hierarchy, meaningful variety. | Random clutter or repeated-looking scenes must not appear. |
| **4. Witness Brief System** | “I know what is waiting for me when I return.” | Choice burden → calm ritual. | One truthful current Brief/resume proposition. | Daily task pressure, FOMO, or competing recommendation labels. |
| **5. Witness Record Evolution** | “I have my own Witness Record.” | Isolated rounds → personal continuity. | Moments/history/familiarity feel remembered. | Badge wall, score dashboard, artificial grind, or external judgment. |
| **6. Premium Presentation Layer** | “This feels premium.” | Functional → tactile/intentional. | Calm atmosphere, coherent motion, sound, typography, evidence polish. | Decorative noise, inaccessible effects, or a cinematic promise gameplay cannot support. |
| **7. Content Expansion Framework** | “There is always something new to notice.” | Familiarity → fresh surprise. | New scenes/templates preserve a recognizable witness grammar. | Content quantity should not lower fairness/quality or become an endless checklist. |
| **8. Device and Performance Excellence** | “It feels perfect.” | Caution → trust. | No crop, lag, tap error, audio glitch, save risk, or accessibility compromise. | Device-specific unfairness must not be normalized as user error. |
| **9. Witness Threads Preparation** | “There is something deeper here.” | Ordinary recall → retrospective curiosity. | Initially little/no visible change; future connection capability is quiet. | No story menu, campaign, chapter, task, notification, or obligation. |
| **10. Flagship Completion Release** | “This has become a complete premium experience.” | Curiosity → attachment. | Store promise, first scene, reveal, brief, record, performance, and content all align. | Late feature additions must not destabilize the coherent release. |

# First-session emotional arc at flagship completion

```text
Invitation
“I can start without effort.”
        ↓
Observation
“Something in this moment matters.”
        ↓
Recall
“This is my best read.”
        ↓
Reveal
“Oh. That was always there.”
        ↓
Record
“This moment is part of my history.”
        ↓
Return
“I want another one.”
```

This arc is successful for both a correct answer and a fair miss. A miss changes the feeling from validation to realization; it must never change it to failure.

# Experience guardrails

A release is moving perception in the wrong direction if player feedback shifts toward:

- “This feels like a memory test.”
- “There are too many things to choose.”
- “I do not know why I should return.”
- “I skipped the reveal because it only tells me my score.”
- “I am worried about losing progress/streak.”
- “The new scenes are just more of the same.”
- “The story wants me to keep up.”
- “It looks polished, but I do not understand what I am doing.”

The roadmap must respond by restoring clarity and evidence quality, not by adding more features.
