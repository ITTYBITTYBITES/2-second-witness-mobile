# Flagship Target Experience — Two Second Witness

**Destination:** a premium, private, daily observation experience built around one exceptional Witness Moment.
**Core promise:** *“This was always there. You just didn’t notice it.”*

---

# 1. Product destination

Two Second Witness is not a collection of brain games, a score pursuit, or a traditional puzzle campaign. It is a product of brief ordinary scenes that become unexpectedly meaningful when the player looks again.

The flagship experience is:

```text
Witness Brief
→ Scene Investigation Witness Moment
→ Observation
→ Recall
→ Evidence Reveal
→ Witness Record
→ clean return or closure
```

The player opens because a moment worth noticing is ready. They leave with a detail, relation, or scene memory that has become more legible than it was seconds earlier.

## Emotional contract

| The product must create | The product must avoid |
|---|---|
| Curiosity | Testing pressure |
| Discovery | Judgment |
| Realization | Generic score feedback |
| Satisfaction | Punishing failure language |
| A desire for another scene | Obligation, streak anxiety, or endless task lists |

The desired reaction is:

> “How did I miss that?”
>
> “That was right in front of me.”
>
> “I want to see another one.”

# 2. First session: first 60 seconds

## 0–10 seconds: invitation and trust

**Introduction:** After required platform/publisher/privacy behavior, the player receives one simple witness invitation:

> **One moment. One detail. Did you catch it?**

They understand that the app is private/offline/no-account and that it is asking for attention, not an evaluation.

**Emotional goal:** calm curiosity.
**Visual goal:** quiet brand frame, no dashboard, no catalog, no rank.
**Interaction goal:** one clear continuation.
**Retention goal:** the player reaches a real moment quickly enough to judge the product by its core, not its menus.

## 10–20 seconds: the witness contract

One line explains the loop:

> **Study the scene. It will disappear. We will show you the evidence.**

No long tutorial, mechanic taxonomy, or multiple optional paths appear. Scene Investigation becomes the intentional first family; companion-family tutorials remain available only when the player later chooses those modes.

**Emotional goal:** readiness, not anxiety.
**Visual goal:** scene identity is introduced without revealing a target.
**Interaction goal:** player understands that broad observation matters.
**Retention goal:** establish the fair-reveal promise before a miss can occur.

## 20–35 seconds: first Witness Moment observation

A novice-safe ordinary scene appears—drawn from the current Scene Investigation world but deliberately constrained as a first-moment asset.

- First exposure: **4.0 seconds** before accessibility multiplier.
- One clear anchor surface and three readable scan zones.
- Six to eight question-eligible objects; minimal decoration.
- No target cue, no target category, no score, no competing UI.
- A restrained peripheral time cue.

**Emotional goal:** “Something in this normal moment may matter.”
**Visual goal:** ordinary world feels tactile/readable/premium.
**Interaction goal:** player scans, not taps.
**Retention goal:** make attention itself feel active and satisfying.

## 35–45 seconds: recall and witness call

The scene conceals for a short neutral memory beat. One direct factual question appears with clear choices. The player makes a single witness call.

- No trick wording.
- No multiple questions.
- No profile/stat interruption.
- Large readable/tappable answers.
- No narrative clue task.

**Emotional goal:** honest commitment.
**Visual goal:** calm question state with no lost scene context implied.
**Interaction goal:** one clear answer.
**Retention goal:** player feels the consequence of their attention without feeling judged.

## 45–60 seconds: evidence reveal and first record

The original scene returns first. Then the exact target/relation is highlighted and explained. Only after the player understands the truth does a small Witness Record acknowledgement appear.

- Correct: “You caught it.”
- Missed: “A detail escaped you.”
- Both: factual evidence explanation in the scene.
- One primary next action: next Witness Moment/current Brief.
- Secondary actions are quiet and optional.

**Emotional goal:** discovery or validation.
**Visual goal:** evidence in context, not a score card.
**Interaction goal:** player can continue or leave satisfied.
**Retention goal:** the first reveal creates voluntary desire for a second scene.

# 3. Returning user target experience

## The opening state

A returning player should not ask, “Which feature should I use?” They should understand immediately:

> **A moment worth noticing is ready.**

The Home experience becomes the doorway to one state-aware Witness Brief:

| Player state | Primary proposition |
|---|---|
| New player | Begin the first Witness Moment. |
| Unfinished brief | Continue the brief when ready. |
| Returning after completion | A fresh Witness Brief is ready. |
| Intentionally browsing | Explore the Library as a secondary choice. |
| Accessibility/comfort preference | Brief resolves timing/presentation automatically and fairly. |

## Witness Brief

A Brief is a finite, optional session unit. It is not a task, notification, or streak obligation.

- It leads with Scene Investigation.
- It may include a companion mode only when variation supports freshness, preference, or accessibility.
- It has a natural stopping point and safe resume state.
- Missing a day never creates loss, debt, or catch-up.
- It never says “finish the story” or “do not break your streak.”

## Scene and recall

The standard mature Witness Moment moves toward the signature **2.0-second** observation rhythm while maintaining independent difficulty axes and Comfortable Timing. A returning player recognizes the scene grammar: anchors, zones, ordinary objects, unknown question, one fair call.

**Emotional goal:** focused anticipation.
**Visual goal:** scene is the hero.
**Interaction goal:** frictionless one-answer response.
**Retention goal:** the player trusts that the next moment will be fresh but fair.

## Evidence reveal

The reveal is the reason the player stays. A correct answer and a missed answer are both treated as evidence experiences. The scene reappears, the detail becomes visible, and the explanation makes the moment understandable.

**Emotional goal:** “Now I see it.”
**Visual goal:** context first, evidence second, text third, progress last.
**Interaction goal:** player may revisit evidence/archive or move on without pressure.
**Retention goal:** a miss still creates curiosity for another scene.

## Witness Record

The Witness Record is a private archive of moments, scene familiarity, and personal discovery—not a game menu of badges and scores.

It answers only a few useful questions:

- What have I witnessed recently?
- Which ordinary worlds am I becoming familiar with?
- What moments were worth remembering?
- Is there a current Brief to resume?

Ranks, achievements, collections, and detailed stats remain supporting/optional evidence, not the reason to open the app.

# 4. Flagship design goals

## Emotional goals

- **Before scene:** anticipation without fear.
- **During observation:** focused attention, not frantic search.
- **At recall:** an honest witness call, not a test answer.
- **At reveal:** realization, whether correct or missed.
- **At return:** a gentle pull toward another moment, not obligation.
- **Over time:** attachment to a personal archive of witnessed ordinary worlds.

## Visual goals

- Dark, quiet product frame; warm, readable ordinary scene stage.
- Eye/witness identity used sparingly as a frame, not a decorative overlay.
- Scene context always more important than timer/chrome.
- Evidence annotations precise, accessible, and restrained.
- Typography optimized for scene/question/explanation comprehension.
- No unsupported noir/campaign promise; atmosphere is observational, not plot-first.

## Interaction goals

- One primary action at every state.
- One question per Witness Moment.
- No configuration burden for timing/difficulty/selection in normal play.
- Manual exploration always available but secondary.
- Every action remains large, safe-area-aware, keyboard/touch accessible.
- Reduced Motion/audio-off/high contrast/comfort modes retain equivalent meaning and progress.

## Retention goals

- First reveal leads to a voluntary second moment.
- A returning user has one understandable current Brief.
- Freshness comes from content, evidence, and fair variation—not manipulation.
- The Record creates quiet personal continuity.
- Future Witness Threads, if ever approved, deepen hindsight without becoming a story obligation.

---

# 5. Flagship completion test

The target experience is achieved only when first-time and returning players can say:

1. “I look at a scene, it disappears, then I answer what I noticed.”
2. “It showed me why the answer was right.”
3. “A miss felt fair because I could see what I missed.”
4. “I knew what I could do when I opened the app.”
5. “I wanted another scene, not another reward.”
6. “My record feels like my history in this game, not a score report.”

No visual polish, content volume, or store asset can substitute for these outcomes.
