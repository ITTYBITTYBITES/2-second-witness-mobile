# Witness Threads Concept

**Status:** Directional concept only. Do not implement during Flagship Reconstruction.
**Recommendation:** **Document now; conditionally prototype later** after the Witness Moment demonstrates retention and fair-reveal value with real players.

---

# Core concept

A **Witness Thread** is a hidden, editorial connection among separate ordinary Witness Moments. It is not a plot the player advances. It is a relationship the player gradually recognizes after they have encountered enough evidence across otherwise complete, independent scenes.

The player may first see:

- a folded transit map beside an office notebook;
- the same map among items on a travel desk;
- a matching marked corner near a garden bench.

None of those moments require the player to remember the map to answer their scene question. Each scene remains a fair standalone Witness Moment. Only later does the archive or a secondary reveal make the connection legible:

> **This detail appeared before.**

The player is not asked to solve a story. They discover that their attention has left a trail.

---

# Why this is not Story Mode

Witness Threads must not introduce the structures that turn a daily observation ritual into a traditional story product.

| Story Mode pattern | Witness Thread alternative |
|---|---|
| Chapters or campaign order | Independent moments encountered through normal play. |
| A plot to complete | A connection that may become visible in hindsight. |
| Named protagonists to follow | Anonymous material traces, places, routines, and visual echoes. |
| Missions or quests | No assigned narrative tasks; the normal scene question remains the only task. |
| Progress bars or completion states | No visible thread count, percentage, locked episode, or “next chapter.” |
| Narrative rewards | The connection itself is the reward: a new way to remember what was already seen. |
| Mandatory catch-up | No missed thread is lost, no schedule is broken, and no session is required. |
| Fictional world as attraction | Ordinary observed moments remain the attraction. |

A thread may imply that objects, routines, or places have history. It must never require the player to follow a character, decode lore, or finish a narrative obligation.

---

# Why the layer should remain invisible initially

The flagship product must first prove a simpler promise:

```text
Scene Investigation → Observation → Recall → Evidence Reveal → Witness Record
```

If thread language appears before a player trusts that loop, it creates the wrong first question:

> “What am I supposed to collect or solve?”

instead of:

> “What mattered in that moment?”

The narrative layer should therefore be **latent**:

- no thread label at first launch;
- no narrative menu/tab;
- no teaser silhouettes, locked slots, or “coming next” message;
- no instruction to look for recurring details;
- no change to the fairness contract of the current scene question.

A player first needs to enjoy noticing one scene. Only after repeated normal Witness Moments can a connection become a quiet bonus interpretation of what they have already seen.

---

# Discovery replaces obligation

## The desired thought

> **“I wonder what I have already seen that I did not understand yet.”**

## The thought to avoid

> **“I need to finish the story.”**

A connection should surface because enough independent evidence exists, not because the player chose a narrative task. It should be possible to ignore it without losing access, progress, fairness, or the day’s Witness Brief.

### Discovery rules

1. **The normal Witness Moment always comes first.** The per-scene question and evidence reveal resolve fully before a larger connection appears.
2. **No perfect-play gate.** A player does not need a flawless score, speed, or streak to be allowed to discover a thread.
3. **No schedule gate.** A player does not need to return on a particular day or in a prescribed order.
4. **No completion mandate.** A player may never see every thread and still have the full flagship experience.
5. **No hidden scoring task.** Recurring details are not a second answer the player can fail.
6. **No explanation overload.** The connection gives one new fact or visual relationship, then leaves room for reflection.

---

# Connection types that fit the product

A thread should be built from **material and environmental echoes**, not plot exposition.

| Connection type | What repeats | Why it fits Witness Moments | Risk to guard against |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Object journey** | One ordinary object appears in several places/states. | Encourages a second look at familiar material evidence. | Do not make it a named character’s possession or required target every time. |
| **Routine echo** | A recurring work/travel/garden habit appears across scenes. | Lets ordinary places feel quietly related. | Do not invent a life story the player must decode. |
| **Visual motif** | Pattern, paper edge, plant marking, tool arrangement, or color/shape relationship recurs. | Can be noticed visually without text or lore. | Never rely on color-only or overly subtle “Easter egg” clues. |
| **Spatial echo** | An object appears in a new relation to a familiar anchor. | Reinforces scene observation grammar and evidence reveals. | The link must not require remembering inaccessible precision details. |
| **Evidence reframe** | A detail that was incidental in one moment becomes meaningful in a later reveal. | Creates the desired hindsight feeling. | Do not make earlier scenes feel incomplete or unfair in retrospect. |

The best thread is mundane enough to be believable and specific enough to create an “oh, I have seen that before” response.

---

# How Witness Threads support product identity

The flagship promise is:

> **“I noticed something others missed.”**

Threads can deepen that promise in a non-competitive way. They give the player a new kind of noticing:

- not only *what was in this scene*;
- but *how this scene quietly related to another moment I already witnessed*.

The connection must remain a reflection on attention, not proof that the player has superior knowledge. Even if the player did not consciously notice an early echo, the later reveal should feel generous:

> “That detail was present before. Now I see why it stayed with me.”

---

# Critical evaluation

## Does it strengthen the Witness Moment?

**Potentially, yes.** It can make evidence reveals more memorable, make ordinary scene worlds feel coherent, and create long-term curiosity using scenes the player already experienced.

**Only if:** the per-scene observation/reveal is already satisfying without the thread. A thread cannot repair a weak first Witness Moment.

## Does it risk turning the product into a detective game?

**Yes, materially.** The words “case,” “clue,” “solve,” and “story” can pull the product toward campaigns, suspects, objectives, and fiction-first play.

**Mitigation:** use material echoes; avoid named characters, crimes, mystery exposition, missions, visible narrative menus, and required resolution. Prefer “connection” or “thread” internally and player-facing only after a connection has surfaced.

## Does it increase content requirements too much?

**Yes, if every scene must carry narrative burden.** Connected content requires recurring assets, continuity QA, ordering controls, archive presentation, and new fairness checks.

**Mitigation:** author a small number of high-quality threads across existing scene worlds. Most moments remain unthreaded, complete, and valuable. Thread content must reuse the same scene, truth-graph, and reveal pipeline rather than demand a second content production system.

## Does it create curiosity or obligation?

It can create either.

- **Curiosity** comes from a quiet retrospective connection, no count, no deadline, and no requirement to act.
- **Obligation** comes from visible progress, locked chapters, named cases, notifications, missed-day consequences, or a “finish this” instruction.

The design must choose curiosity every time.

## Is the emotional payoff stronger than the complexity introduced?

**Unknown today.** The payoff can be unusually strong—“that object mattered more than I knew”—but only if players already care about the underlying scenes and reveals. The additional content/QA complexity is not justified until the flagship produces voluntary replay and players naturally remember details across sessions.

---

# Recommendation

## Now: Document only

Do **not** build thread state, narrative UI, Cases, archive connections, or a new content system during Flagship Reconstruction. The current priorities remain:

1. intentional first Witness Moment;
2. fair Scene Investigation scene quality;
3. evidence reveal clarity;
4. simple Witness Record; and
5. real-player/device validation.

## Later: Prototype one invisible thread, conditionally

After retention/fairness validation, prototype **one three-scene material thread** in a controlled research build. It must use existing scene-generation/evidence systems, appear only after the third independent moment, and have no completion UI or notification pressure.

## Do not build as a broad product layer until

- players voluntarily return for normal Witness Moments;
- players remember/refer to prior scenes without prompting;
- evidence reveals are already the emotional reward;
- the prototype creates curiosity rather than confusion or completion pressure;
- the content/QA cost is sustainable without reducing standalone scene quality.
