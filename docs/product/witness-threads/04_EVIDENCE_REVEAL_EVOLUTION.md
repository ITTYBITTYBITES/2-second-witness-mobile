# Evidence Reveal Evolution — When a Detail Gains Meaning

**Purpose:** extend the normal evidence reveal only after its standalone truth has landed.
**Player outcome:** “This detail mattered more than I realized.”

---

# Non-negotiable hierarchy

A thread must never replace or obscure the per-scene reveal.

```text
1. Resolve this scene’s truth.
2. Let the player understand the current evidence.
3. Only then, optionally surface a prior connection.
```

The normal Witness Moment remains complete if the connection does not appear, is skipped, or is ignored.

---

# Baseline versus evolved reveal

| Reveal layer | Standalone Witness Moment | Witness Thread eligible moment |
|---|---|---|
| Context return | Original scene returns unmarked. | Same. No thread signal yet. |
| Evidence focus | Current target/relation is highlighted. | Same. Current truth remains primary. |
| Explanation | One factual sentence explains the current answer. | Same sentence first. |
| Emotional recognition | “You caught it” / “A detail escaped you.” | Same. No altered scoring or judgment. |
| Optional second beat | None required. | A quiet connection may surface after the normal reveal settles. |
| Record update | Current moment is saved. | Current moment is saved; related prior evidence may be available in archive. |

---

# When a connection may appear

A connection appears only when all conditions hold:

1. The player has encountered the minimum three valid thread beats.
2. The current scene’s normal evidence reveal is complete and readable.
3. The connection has a clear visual/factual basis across scenes.
4. Surfacing it does not interrupt an unfinished Witness Brief or override a player-selected mode.
5. Accessibility settings can present the relationship equivalently.
6. The connection is not merely a repeated asset with no reframe or emotional meaning.

## When it should not appear

- On the first or second thread beat.
- Before the current question is explained.
- During the observation timer, question response, or answer commit.
- As a push notification or launch interruption.
- In a way that makes a missed earlier detail feel like a retroactive failure.
- More than once in a short session unless research proves the player welcomes it.
- If current scene fairness, visual readability, or core result behavior is compromised.

---

# Connection reveal sequence

## Beat 1 — Current truth settles

Use the standard evidence reveal exactly as specified for the flagship:

- scene context returns;
- current relevant object/relation is highlighted;
- factual explanation appears;
- correct/missed response receives calm recognition.

**No thread cue appears here.**

## Beat 2 — Quiet pause

After the player has had time to read current evidence, allow a brief calm pause. This is not suspense. It gives the normal reveal dignity and creates room for the additional meaning.

## Beat 3 — Visual callback

A small related-evidence signal enters from the archive language, not from a game-show notification:

- a prior scene reference appears as a quiet visual card/crop;
- the same object/motif is marked in both contexts;
- a restrained trace connects current and prior evidence;
- the current scene remains dominant.

## Beat 4 — Factual connection line

Use one concise sentence, for example:

> **This folded map appeared in two earlier moments you witnessed.**

or:

> **You saw this marked corner at the Travel Desk and the Garden Bench.**

No lore explanation, character name, plot revelation, “case solved,” or progress count follows.

## Beat 5 — Open archive or continue

The player may optionally view connected evidence in their Witness Record. The primary continuation remains the Witness Brief/next normal moment. There is no “follow thread” task.

# Animation language

## Desired feeling: memory aligning

The connection should look like separate visual memories briefly finding a relationship.

### Use

- Current scene stays stable while prior reference fades/settles in.
- A thin, calm trace or shared framing connects the repeated evidence.
- The recurring detail receives the same evidence treatment in each scene reference.
- Motion is short, purposeful, and subordinate to readable context.
- Reduced Motion uses stable side-by-side/stacked evidence with no dependency on animation.

### Avoid

- Cinematic flashback montage.
- Camera rushes, dramatic zooms, jump cuts, or thriller stingers.
- A detective-board red-string effect.
- A large modal interrupting the result.
- Confetti, reward particles, chest opening, badge flip, or unlock fanfare.
- Any visual effect that makes the player believe they must act now.

---

# Sound and haptics

## Sound motif

A thread can use a subtle repeated sonic motif: a small tonal interval or textural cue that has previously appeared unobtrusively around related scenes. At convergence it resolves gently.

The sound should feel like recognition, not an alarm or a victory prize.

## Rules

- Play only after the normal reveal explanation is readable.
- No sound is necessary to understand the connection.
- Respect all volume/mute settings.
- Use no more than one light optional haptic acknowledgment.
- Audio-off and Reduced Motion must still communicate the full relationship through stable visual evidence and text.

The existing AudioService, settings, and route-based cue system are sufficient foundations if this layer is ever prototyped.

---

# Typography and copy

## Voice

Connection copy should be observational, factual, and open-ended:

- “This detail appeared before.”
- “You witnessed this object in another moment.”
- “These moments share the same marked map.”
- “A connection surfaced in your record.”

## Avoid

- “Case complete.”
- “You unlocked the next chapter.”
- “Follow the clue.”
- “Solve the mystery.”
- “You found all evidence.”
- “You missed a collectible.”
- any implication that the player is now obligated to continue.

## Information intentionally withheld

The product should withhold interpretation, not evidence.

It may show that an object/motif recurred. It should not explain why a fictional person moved it, what plot it proves, or where the thread ends. The player supplies the meaning through remembered scenes.

This restraint is essential. Over-explanation turns an emergent connection into exposition; under-explanation without visible evidence turns it into confusing lore.

---

# Correct and missed response behavior

## Correct current response

The connection can acknowledge continuity after validating the player’s current observation:

> **You caught it. This marked map has appeared in earlier moments you witnessed.**

The player’s correctness is about the current scene, not proof that they solved the thread.

## Missed current response

First explain the current scene normally. Then surface the connection without converting it into a double miss:

> **A detail escaped you. The map was beside the notebook.**
>
> **This map appeared in earlier moments you witnessed.**

The later line is a gift of perspective, not a statement that the player missed hidden narrative content.

---

# Reveal evolution acceptance criteria

A thread-aware reveal is acceptable only when:

- [ ] The player can explain the current scene answer before the connection appears.
- [ ] The connection has visible evidence in every referenced scene.
- [ ] The player does not mistake the connection for a task, unlock, or missed collectible.
- [ ] Correctness and thread discovery remain separate concepts.
- [ ] The connection is understandable with sound off, Reduced Motion, High Contrast, and text scaling.
- [ ] It adds curiosity without slowing ordinary result closure excessively.
- [ ] It does not obscure the primary next action or the option to stop cleanly.
- [ ] Research shows the emotional payoff is stronger than the interruption/complexity it adds.
