# Core Product Identity Report — Two Second Witness

**Direction phase:** Core Experience Discovery
**Foundation:** Product Reconstruction Discovery reports, 2026-07-15
**Decision type:** Product direction, not UI implementation

---

# Directional conclusion

**Two Second Witness should become a premium, offline daily observation game about catching the one meaningful detail in a vanished ordinary moment—and receiving the satisfying proof of what you saw or missed.**

It should not become a generic collection of memory mini-games, a cognitive assessment, a dashboard of self-improvement metrics, or a cinematic detective story the player cannot actually play. Its strongest form is a calm, tense, fair **witness-moment ritual**: observe, commit, discover, and return with a slightly sharper in-game eye.

## Core promise

The player is offered a brief moment that matters. They do not know exactly what will be asked, so they must genuinely observe. After committing to one answer, the game proves the truth through a visual reveal. Success feels like sharp noticing; a miss feels like a surprising second look rather than failure.

### Single-sentence product identity

> **The user opens this product because they want to catch the one detail that matters in a fleeting moment—and enjoy discovering what they missed.**

## What desire it satisfies

| Player desire/emotion | The product’s answer |
|---|---|
| **Curiosity** | “What did I miss in that moment?” |
| **A short satisfying challenge** | A complete observe → decide → reveal arc in seconds/minutes, not an open-ended task list. |
| **Competence without pressure** | The player can feel observant and improve within the game without being judged, diagnosed, or ranked against strangers. |
| **A small daily ritual** | A fresh, finite witness brief creates a reason to return without demanding a long session. |
| **Trust and calm** | Offline, no account, no ads, local progress, accessibility controls, and clear fairness make the experience safe to revisit. |

## What makes it different from existing puzzle apps

The differentiator is not “memory” alone. Many apps flash items and ask questions. Two Second Witness has a stronger combination:

1. **A witness fantasy grounded in ordinary moments.** The player is not completing an abstract test; they are noticing what mattered in a scene.
2. **Fairness made visible.** The result reveal returns to the evidence and teaches the miss.
3. **The moment disappears before the question.** This turns attention into the actual skill and makes the player’s first impression consequential.
4. **A personal, private record rather than a public contest.** Progress means familiarity and mastery inside the game, not an external claim about intelligence or real-world ability.
5. **A consistent product ritual across varied mechanics.** Companion modes can refresh observation without replacing the central witness experience.

# The product hierarchy to adopt

## 1. Core: Witness Moments

A **Witness Moment** is the canonical unit of the product:

```text
A brief ordinary moment
→ attention without knowing the exact question
→ one committed observation decision
→ evidence returned and explained
→ a clear next moment or a satisfying stop
```

Scene Investigation is the strongest present implementation of this unit. It should define the product language, marketing promise, onboarding, daily ritual, and mastery model.

## 2. Companion experiences: Observation variations

Flash Words, Spot the Difference, Object Recall, and Pattern Recall should be treated as variations that serve one of three purposes:

- refresh attention and prevent the central loop from becoming monotonous;
- offer different accessibility/comfort preferences;
- broaden what “being a witness” can mean without requiring a new product identity.

They should not all be assumed to deserve identical prominence in the first session or Home hierarchy. Their future roles must be confirmed through player research.

## 3. Product ritual: The Daily Witness Brief

The desired return unit is a **finite daily witness brief**, not an endless feed and not a stack of generic menus. The existing Daily Witness Program, recommendations, runtime, content tags, and progress systems are usable building blocks.

For the ritual to be meaningful, it must answer a player’s implicit question on open:

> “What is today’s moment for me to notice?”

A daily brief should be coherent and intentional. It cannot merely label any balanced recommendation “today.”

## 4. Long-term relationship: The Witness Record

Long-term engagement should be framed as a growing record of moments encountered and observation patterns mastered **inside the game**. It should make players feel familiar with the world and proud of their consistency, not burden them with opaque counters.

The current mastery, history, rank, favorites, and Program data can support this direction. They need a clear hierarchy and player meaning before they become more prominent.

# Non-goals for the strongest product

The current system can technically support many directions. The following directions would dilute the core promise and should not guide the next implementation phase without new strategic evidence:

- **Assessment/brain-training claims.** The product must not imply diagnosis, IQ, therapeutic effect, or measurable real-world cognitive improvement.
- **A generic mini-game anthology.** Five mechanics alone are not an identity.
- **A full narrative detective game.** The trailer’s noir/investigator language is atmosphere, not current playable story content. Building a story layer only to justify marketing would be scope expansion, not core-product clarification.
- **Leaderboard-first competition.** Fast observation and private fair reveals are the current strengths; global performance comparison would alter the emotional contract.
- **Reward/economy clutter.** Coins, inventories, battle-pass-like pressure, and forced collection would distract from the reveal as the reward.
- **More Challenge Types before learning from the current five.** The portfolio is sufficient to determine what players actually value.

---

# Product-direction test

A future feature, screen, program, challenge type, or marketing claim should pass this test:

1. Does it make the player more eager to notice the next moment?
2. Does it strengthen fair observation and the evidence reveal?
3. Does it clarify the witness identity rather than adding a second product?
4. Does it preserve a short, calm, private, accessible session?
5. Can it be explained without implying assessment, pressure, or a system the player must manage?

If the answer is no, it is not part of the core product direction.
