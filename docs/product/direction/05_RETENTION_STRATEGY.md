# Retention Strategy — Two Second Witness

**Direction phase:** Core Experience Discovery
**Position:** retention should come from curiosity, fair mastery, and a private ritual—not pressure, competition, or metric accumulation.

---

# 1. Retention thesis

A player should return because every completed Witness Moment leaves behind an unresolved, positive thought:

> “Next time, I might catch it.”

That thought is created by the pairing of a brief unknown observation task and a fair evidence reveal. The product’s retention strategy should deepen this feeling over time rather than distract from it with unrelated loops.

---

# 2. Engagement mechanisms: what exists, what is missing, and direction

| Mechanism | What exists now | Directional role | What is missing or unproven | What should not be added now |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Curiosity** | Unknown questions, seeded scenes, visual changes, varied content, evidence reveals. | **Primary retention driver.** | Proof that reveals create “one more” behavior; thematic coherence across daily sessions. | Artificial mystery boxes, opaque randomness, cliffhangers without playable payoff. |
| **Progress** | Levels, ranks, progress points, mastery, history, achievements, collections, Programs. | **Supporting driver.** Progress should remember meaningful play. | A clear player interpretation/hierarchy; evidence that players care about each signal. | More currencies, parallel meters, streak pressure, or opaque gates. |
| **Mastery** | Family mastery/confidence, difficulty/exposure policies, template variety. | **Primary supporting driver.** Mastery should mean familiarity with fair observation inside the game. | Human proof that adaptation feels earned/fair; a clear flagship-centered mastery story. | Claims of real-world cognitive improvement or speed-only expertise. |
| **Personal improvement** | Private records, accuracy, fastest response, history, ranks. | **Careful secondary driver.** Players may feel sharper in the game. | Safe language and user understanding that avoids assessment framing. | Diagnostic scores, IQ-style labels, prescriptive self-improvement claims. |
| **Collection** | Derived collections, achievements, scene/template content, favorites. | **Optional discovery layer.** | Whether players value collecting moments/content rather than counters. | Inventory economy, loot, forced completion, scarcity pressure. |
| **Competition** | None. | **Not a priority.** | No evidence it would preserve the product’s fair/private feeling. | Global leaderboards, rank anxiety, time-limited score contests. |
| **Discovery** | Library, five families, 20 templates, tutorial replay, Programs, favorites. | **Important after core comprehension.** | Whether players understand family differences and want to explore. | Presenting all options as mandatory choices on first launch. |
| **Social sharing** | No in-app sharing system; scenes/reveals are visually shareable in principle. | **Potential future amplifier, not core loop.** | What players would be proud to share and how to preserve privacy/fairness. | Social feed, forced friend systems, spoilers, competitive comparison. |
| **Emotional attachment** | Witness language, eye motif, ordinary scenes, local record, audio/visual atmosphere. | **Long-term differentiator.** | A coherent tone between gameplay, Home, and trailer; player language/attachment research. | Lore expansion used only to compensate for a weak core loop. |

# 3. Recommended retention model

## Layer 1 — Immediate: the fair reveal

Every round must create a meaningful payoff. The reveal should make the player either proud of a real observation or delighted by the missed detail. If this layer fails, no progression system can sustainably compensate.

**Measure:** voluntary next-round choice, fair-miss reaction, reveal dwell/attention, player explanation of what happened.

## Layer 2 — Session: the finite Witness Brief

A player should know they can complete a satisfying small unit in a few minutes. A finite daily brief provides closure and a natural return point without requiring endless play.

**Measure:** start/finish/resume rate, voluntary extra round rate, perceived session length, comprehension of the brief’s purpose.

## Layer 3 — Week: fresh moments in familiar worlds

Players should return to see a new evidence situation in a world/mode they recognize. Scene Investigation’s ordinary settings are well suited to this: familiarity reduces learning friction; procedural composition keeps details fresh.

**Measure:** perceived repetition after 5/20/50 rounds, recognition of scene worlds, voluntary family preference, novelty/fatigue comments.

## Layer 4 — Month: a private Witness Record

A personal record should let players see a history of moments, favorite experiences, and in-game mastery without turning the product into a life-performance tracker.

**Measure:** Profile visits, recall of meaningful milestones, understanding of mastery/rank, emotional response to past moments.

# 4. What the current systems should do in the retention model

## Recommendations

**Keep:** the generic recommendation architecture, Continue priority, recent-family knowledge, family weights, catalog locks, and deterministic daily feature.

**Transform directionally:** make recommendation semantics singular and honest. The product should not label a generic balanced recommendation as “today” while also maintaining a separate daily feature. A player should be able to explain why the offered moment is current.

## Programs

**Keep:** finite run state, resume, selection policies, focus tags, favorites, schedule support, and runtime integration.

**Transform directionally:** Programs should earn visibility only when they express a meaningful session intent. The central daily brief should be more important than a catalog of nine program types until research proves that players want/understand multiple curated paths.

## Progression

**Keep:** witness progress, per-family mastery, history, favorite types, and rank metadata.

**Transform directionally:** make progress answer one human question: *“What am I becoming familiar with in this game?”* Achievements and collections should support this answer, not multiply it.

## Achievements and collections

**Keep:** data-driven definitions and persistence; they are useful optional recognition infrastructure.

**Transform directionally:** treat the 26-achievement catalog as a background system until research identifies a small set of milestones players genuinely value.

## Content and families

**Keep:** seeded generation, template/content ownership, anti-repeat logic, and multiple modes.

**Transform directionally:** let the flagship scene experience define return identity. Companion modes should refresh a routine with a reason, not merely maximize catalog rotation.

# 5. Metrics and research signals to collect before optimizing retention

The current local-only analytics buffer is not a sufficient product-learning system. Before any retention optimization, gather consent-appropriate research evidence.

## First session

- Time from meaningful start to first Witness Moment.
- Tutorial comprehension without coaching.
- First correct/miss reaction.
- Whether the player views the evidence reveal.
- Voluntary second-round choice.
- What the player believes the app is for.

## Session behavior

- Number of Witness Moments completed before voluntary stop.
- Chosen post-result action: next, retry, Home, Library, Program.
- Chosen family after first exposure.
- Whether Programs are started/completed/resumed intentionally.
- Whether progress surfaces are noticed or ignored.

## Longitudinal behavior

- Return on another day/week, but do not reduce it to a punitive streak.
- Daily brief start/finish/resume patterns.
- Family preference and avoidance.
- Repetition/fatigue points by template/content type.
- Accessibility setting changes and outcomes.
- Player descriptions of fairness, tension, and personal improvement.

## Qualitative signals that matter more than raw frequency

- “I knew I could have caught that.”
- “Show me another one.”
- “I like this scene/mode because…”
- “I came back to see today’s…”
- “I understand what my progress means.”

Signals such as “I feel stupid,” “that was random,” “I do not know what I should play,” or “this feels like a test” are core-product warnings.

---

# 6. Retention features to explicitly defer

The product does not need the following to discover its strongest version:

- Global leaderboard or competitive ranking.
- Multiplayer, friend invites, social feed, or chat.
- Currency, shop, loot, energy, battle pass, or forced daily streak recovery.
- Notifications designed around loss aversion.
- New planned Challenge Types before existing 20/50-round research is complete.
- Narrative/live-event infrastructure meant to manufacture attachment.
- Cloud/account work before validating that local-only progress is a retention problem for the actual audience.

The strongest retention strategy is a better reason to notice the next moment, not a larger set of obligations.
