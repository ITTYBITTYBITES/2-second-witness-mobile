# Top 10 Product Decisions We Must Make Before Implementation

**Direction phase:** Core Experience Discovery
**Rule:** These decisions are intentionally unresolved until supported by player, device, and product evidence. They should be made before UI redesign or feature expansion.

---

## 1. What is the product’s non-negotiable core?

**Decision:** Is Two Second Witness fundamentally a scene-based witness game, a daily observation ritual, a varied observation anthology, or another clearly stated experience?

**Current recommendation:** Scene-based Witness Moments are the core; the daily ritual is the return structure; other families are companions.

**Evidence required:** First-session comprehension and voluntary replay across all five families.

---

## 2. Is Scene Investigation truly the flagship?

**Decision:** Should Scene Investigation lead onboarding, Home, daily briefs, marketing, and mastery—or does another existing family earn that role?

**Current recommendation:** Use Scene Investigation as the working flagship hypothesis; make Spot the Difference the primary challenger.

**Evidence required:** Comparative clarity, fair-miss reaction, voluntary replay, 20/50-round freshness, marketing comprehension, and accessibility research.

---

## 3. What does “Two Second” promise?

**Decision:** Is the name literal timing, a brand mood, or a shorthand for brief attention?

**Current reality:** Timing already varies substantially by family and difficulty for fairness.

**Evidence required:** Player expectation testing after hearing the name and playing real rounds; fairness response at actual exposure ranges.

---

## 4. What exactly happens on first launch?

**Decision:** What is the legally compliant, emotionally clear sequence from launch to first result?

**Current reality:** Source can show privacy acknowledgment, title intro tutorial, family tutorial gating, practice, and result; documents do not describe that path consistently.

**Evidence required:** Unmoderated/moderated first-session walkthroughs, privacy comprehension, tutorial comprehension, and drop-off/confusion points.

---

## 5. What is a daily Witness Brief?

**Decision:** Does the product have a true daily ritual? If so, what makes it finite, fresh, resumable, and meaningful?

**Current reality:** There is a deterministic daily feature and a Daily Witness Program, while Home V2 labels a balanced recommendation as today’s experience.

**Evidence required:** Returning-player interviews, daily brief start/finish/resume behavior, comprehension of “today,” and no-pressure response to missed days.

---

## 6. What should progression mean to the player?

**Decision:** Which progress signals deserve meaning: Witness Record, family mastery, rank, achievements, history, favorites, Programs, collections, or a smaller subset?

**Current reality:** All of these are implemented and persisted; their hierarchy is not player-validated.

**Evidence required:** Player recall of milestones, profile interpretation, motivation/avoidance behavior, and willingness to return after both success and failure.

---

## 7. What role does each companion Challenge Type serve?

**Decision:** Which families are essential companions, specialist modes, optional discovery, accessibility alternatives, or candidates for future de-emphasis?

**Current reality:** Five mechanically distinct families have equal catalog status except Pattern Recall’s level gate.

**Evidence required:** Voluntary selection, reasons for preference, fatigue/variety results, identity fit, and accessibility outcomes.

---

## 8. What product promise can marketing honestly make?

**Decision:** How should witness/ordinary-scene play, dark premium art, “What changed?” language, and the cinematic psychological-thriller trailer relate?

**Current reality:** Trailer atmosphere is stronger/more narrative than current playable content.

**Evidence required:** First-impression testing with real gameplay footage, trailer/product expectation comparison, and store-page comprehension.

---

## 9. What is the release-quality bar and supported device scope?

**Decision:** What must pass before the product can claim premium mobile readiness?

**Current reality:** Physical Android boot/device/accessibility/audio/haptic/save/signed-AAB gates remain open; current static verification baselines are partially stale.

**Evidence required:** Complete release checklist, reconciled current test baseline, real device matrix, signed artifact review, and actual save-upgrade tests.

---

## 10. Which architecture is active, and what must be preserved during evolution?

**Decision:** What systems are product-critical, compatibility-only, or retired—and what player-data promise must survive a rebuild?

**Current reality:** The Challenge Family runtime is active and valuable; Foundation-era ExperienceRegistry/legacy Flashword content is mostly dormant; deterministic fixtures remain intentional regression coverage.

**Evidence required:** Dependency/migration map, save schema audit, active route trace, ownership of obsolete test/documentation baselines, and explicit preservation rules.

---

# Order of operations

The first four decisions should be resolved before any redesign work:

1. core product identity;
2. flagship family;
3. first-session flow;
4. daily/return proposition.

Decisions 5–10 then determine progression, portfolio, marketing, release scope, and implementation boundaries. Building new screens, modes, rewards, or narrative content before these choices are evidenced would recreate the current product’s main risk: a technically capable application without one fully proven experience at its center.
