# LIQUID MEMORY V2: THE ALPHA PLAN

The technical architecture is complete. The next 2-4 weeks must focus exclusively on product validation through these strict milestones.

## Milestone 1: Ten Scenarios (Depth, Not Quantity)
Expand from 3 spikes to 10. Do not add new mechanics after this.
- **Goal:** Memory Recall × 3 | Pattern Recognition × 3 | Classification × 2 | Surprise/Novelty × 2
- **Success:** A player can complete 20 loops without seeing the exact same experience repeatedly.

## Milestone 2: The Real Iris (Salience Polish)
Replace the placeholder torus with the final perceptual anchor.
- **Requirements:** Visible < 250ms | Recognizable silhouette | Strong luminance | Click target larger than mesh.
- **Success:** First-time users identify and click it without instruction.

## Milestone 3: Session Tracking (Lightweight Telemetry)
Track the reality of player behavior locally, not on a dashboard.
- **Track:** Session length | Loops completed | Spike completion rate | Error rate per spike.
- **Success:** You can factually answer "what are people actually doing?"

## Milestone 4: First External User (The Silent Test)
Put the Godot build in front of a friend, spouse, or coworker.
- **Instruction:** "Try it." (Then say nothing).
- **Success:** They independently discover the Iris and complete a cognitive loop.

## Milestone 5: Android Build (Hardware Crucible)
Export the APK. Not polished, not optimized, just running on real silicon.
- **Test:** Startup time | Touch accuracy | Screen readability | FPS | Device heat after 15 minutes.
- **Success:** The experience survives and is playable on actual hardware.

## Milestone 6: Retention Review
After 5-10 testers, answer the hard product questions:
1. Which spike is most liked?
2. Which spike is skipped mentally?
3. Does the slingshot matter?
4. Does the tunnel matter?
5. Is the Iris compelling?

---

### WHAT NOT TO DO
- 960 scenario conversion
- Additional universes
- Marketplace assets
- Monetization work
- Progression / Social / Achievement systems

*These are multipliers. Prove the core loop is worth multiplying first.*

**THE SINGLE GOAL:** Can a new user play for five minutes and voluntarily choose to continue?
