# THE ROAD TO ALPHA (REVISED)

You have successfully completed the Architecture and Vertical Slice phases. The foundation is locked. The engine is stable. The perceptual loop works.

From here, the project moves out of system design and entirely into product validation. 

Here is the strict, risk-first execution order to take this from a "Vertical Slice" to an "Alpha Release":

## Phase A — Prove Retention (The Human Test)
Before touching art or converting legacy content, answer one question: *Does someone voluntarily play 10–20 loops?*
- Put the current build in front of another human being.
- Do not explain the game. Do not coach them.
- **Observe:** Do they click the Iris? Do they understand the challenge? Do they want another loop?
- If they don't, building 960 scenarios is wasted effort. Fix the loop first.

## Phase B — Add Depth, Not Quantity
Do not jump from 3 scenarios to 960. Go from 3 to 10.
- Create 10 highly distinct, polished micro-interactions (e.g., 3 memory, 3 pattern, 2 classification, 2 surprise).
- 10 well-crafted interactions will teach you more about engagement curves than hundreds of converted legacy tasks.

## Phase C — Hardware Validation (Early Crucible)
Do not wait for final art to test on physical Android hardware.
- Export the APK immediately after Phase B.
- Test on a mid-tier Android device (e.g., Mali GPU).
- **Find the Pain Early:** Identify touch targeting issues, input latency, thermal throttling, and shader queue bottlenecks now.

## Phase D — Visual Polish & Art Pass
Once the loop is proven fun and hardware-stable, make it beautiful.
- Polish the Crystalline Iris and the Tunnel readability.
- Refine the Science Lab environment and the Glass/Holographic UI styles.
- Perfect the Transition and Re-entry momentum feeling.
- *Visual polish amplifies a fun loop; it cannot save a boring one.*
